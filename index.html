Research Focus

PhD · Cybersecurity & Remote Engineering

My research explores how to secure remote monitoring and control systems built on IoT and IIoT technologies, with emphasis on runtime anomaly detection and policy-enforced, identity-aware architectures.

  • IIoT/ICS testbeds for cybersecurity experimentation – design and evaluation of modular testbeds with MQTT, Node-RED, InfluxDB, and Grafana.
  • Real-time MQTT-based intrusion detection – feature selection, model training, and deployment of ML-based IDS on edge devices.
  • Zero Trust for IoT/IIoT – identity-aware access control, mTLS, and policy enforcement for remote engineering applications.
  • Educational remote labs – integrating secure remote access and monitoring into engineering education environments.

Selected Projects

Implementation & experimentation
  • IIoT MQTT Security Testbed – ESP32 sensors, Raspberry Pi broker, Node-RED orchestration, InfluxDB storage, Grafana dashboards, and real-time ML-based anomaly detection for MQTT traffic.
  • Experimental Research on Cybersecurity in Remote Control Systems – integrating IDS, attack simulation, and policy-based enforcement into remote engineering scenarios.
  • VICSORT-based ICS security lab – use of an ICS testbed for teaching and research on industrial control system security and attack experimentation.
  • Zero Trust for Remote Labs – applying Zero Trust principles to secure online laboratory access and remote engineering platforms.

I am particularly interested in bridging the gap between research prototypes and deployable architectures that can be adopted in both educational and industrial contexts.
